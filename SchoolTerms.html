School Term Programs

Our core programs run according to the school terms. 10-week ‘curriculums’ designed to afford us the time to focus on long-term player development. These programs give us the opportunity to get to know the player and better identify their strengths and weaknesses with the ball.

U7's - U8's: A typical term curriculum has approximately a 70/30 split. 60-70% of our 10-week term sessions are spent working on 1v1 related exercises, ensuring the individual player is on the ball for the maximum amount of time. E.g., dribbling, ball mastery, defending, tricks and turns, as well as space utilisation when attacking. The other 30-40% of our allocated term sessions focus on passing and all it’s aspects. This latter component of the curriculum has a 2 v 1’s and 3 v 2’s flavour to it.

U9's - U12's: A typical term follows a rotational timetable, covering five main modules. (1) Dribbling and Ball Mastery (2) Attacking (3) Passing and Ball Control (4) Possession (5) Defending

. We believe mastering each of these modules best prepares the player for their teenager years when tactics and performance based 11-aside games become the primary focus during player development.

Pricing and Player Commitments

Click on Pricing for up-to-date Academy costs.

All term programs have a minimum commitment of 1 session per week for that term i.e., 10 sessions.

If a player wants to join after the term has started then it is still a minimum commitment of 1 session per week for the remainder of weeks in that school term (pro-rata concept).

Players are to select a minimum of one time slot per week according to their age group. Ages are determined based on the players age as of December EOY.
